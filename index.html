A guide to the genres

My novels live in specific corners of fiction. Before you dive in, here's what each genre actually means — because dark romance is not your average love story.

🥀
Dark Romance
Core Genre

Love stories where the road to happiness is messy, dangerous, or morally complicated. Think obsession, power imbalance, enemies who can't stay away. The feelings are real — the circumstances are not always safe. It's not a red flag, it's the whole genre.

⚠ Contains mature themes
🔥
Smut
Explicit Content

Explicit romantic and sexual content between adult characters. No fade to black — the tension breaks, and it's written out. Some novels are story-first with smut scenes. Others are almost entirely smut. I'll always mark which is which on the card.

🔞 Adults only
💔
Angst & Heartbreak
Emotional

Stories that are heavy on emotion — longing, loss, unrequited love, almost-relationships. The kind that make your chest tight. May or may not have a happy ending. You've been warned.

🫀 Emotionally intense
🏫
Campus Romance
Setting

Set in university — the hallways, the libraries, the parties, the dorms. Usually involves someone you should not be falling for. A professor, a rival, a stranger who sits too close in lecture. Classic setting, messy feelings.

📚 University setting
👁️
Obsession / Stalker
Dark Trope

One character becomes fixated — watching, following, knowing too much. A fantasy trope, not a blueprint. In real life this is dangerous and wrong. In fiction it's a lens for exploring desire, control, and the parts of ourselves we don't say out loud.

⚠ Dark themes — fiction only
⚔️
Enemies to Lovers
Fan Favourite Trope

They hate each other. Or they pretend to. The tension is unbearable, the arguments are foreplay, and when they finally break — it hits differently. One of the most beloved tropes in romance fiction for a reason.

💥 High tension
